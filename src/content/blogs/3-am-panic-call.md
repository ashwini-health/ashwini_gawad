*"Beta, Papa's sugar was 210 fasting today. Should we increase the tablet?"*

If you are an NRI managing your parents' health in India, you know this panic. You log into Google, try to decode the prescription, and attempt to explain glycemic index over a spotty FaceTime call to a cook who is already frying *puris*.

It doesn't work.

After 25 years of clinical practice and managing over 500 cases of uncontrolled Type 2 Diabetes, I see the same pattern in NRI families. The medication is world-class. The intent is there. But the execution is fundamentally broken.

Here is why his HbA1c is stuck at 8.5—and what needs to change today.

### 1. The "Sugar-Free" Deception

Most Indian households believe diabetes management means cutting out table sugar and sweets. That is step zero. 

The real spike is happening from the massive glycemic load of the traditional Indian lunch mechanism:
- 3 Rotis (often pure wheat, low fiber)
- A tiny bowl of *dal* (insufficient protein)
- A large bowl of carb-heavy *sabzi* (potato/peas)

When calculating the total carbohydrate load, this meal acts exactly like a dessert in the bloodstream of a diabetic patient. 

### 2. The Protein Deficit in Vegetarian Elders

Sarcopenia (muscle loss) in aging Indian vegetarians is epidemic. Muscle acts as a sink for glucose. Less muscle means less storage capacity for blood sugar, leading to higher circulating glucose levels.

Most elderly parents consume less than 30g of protein a day. They need closer to 1g per kg of body weight. The morning tea and *khari* biscuit routine is actively harming their insulin sensitivity.

### 3. The Communication Gap with the Cook

You cannot manage a clinical condition with Whatsapp forwards. 

If the person cooking the food does not understand portion control, oil temperature, and the difference between complex and simple carbohydrates, the doctor's prescription is useless. The cook needs a laminated, idiot-proof blueprint, not a general guideline.
